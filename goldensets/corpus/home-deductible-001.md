title: Home Insurance — All-Perils Deductible
---

This section of your home insurance policy defines the all-perils deductible. The all-perils deductible is the amount you pay out of pocket on a covered home claim before your coverage begins to pay.

Your all-perils deductible is $250 per claim. You are responsible for the first $250 of every covered home loss, and the all-perils deductible applies separately to each incident.

An annual aggregate cap of $1,000 limits the total all-perils deductible you can pay across all home claims in one policy year. Selecting a higher all-perils deductible lowers your premium; a lower all-perils deductible raises it.
